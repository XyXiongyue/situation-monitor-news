The Hormuz Threat Oil Markets Are Ignoring

Jun 22, 2026 at 14:40 | Irina Slav

Renewed U.S.-Iran tensions are keeping Hormuz risk elevated, despite ongoing negotiations, with threats of renewed strikes and stalled talks sustaining uncertainty around global oil supply security.

GPS/GNSS spoofing, impaired vessel tracking, sanctions compliance concerns, and voyage verification issues are creating major challenges for shippers, insurers, banks, and traders.

Iran’s new transit permit and insurance regime adds another layer of complexity, while soaring tanker insurance costs and information gaps continue to widen the disconnect between physical oil market risks and relatively calmer futures prices.
