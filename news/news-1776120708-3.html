Ukraine drones ignite Putin's key oil export port as Russia loses 8,000 troops in a week

Mon, 23 Mar 2026 09:10

Vladimir Putin faced a significant setback today as Ukraine launched a devastating strike on Primorsk, his largest oil exporting port in western Russia. Multiple drones targeted the Baltic Sea port, setting the massive hub ablaze.

The port plays a vital role in Putin's hard currency earnings through his infamous sanctions-evading shadow fleet. This comes after a week where Russia hasreportedly lost another 8,000 troops, either killed or injured.

"Firefighting is underway, personnel have been evacuated," reported Alexander Drozdenko, the governor of the Leningrad region. Thedrone strikes caused flightdisruptions in nearby St Petersburg, Russia's second-largest city.

Primorsk, located nearly 700 miles from the closest Ukrainian territory, is Russia's biggest oil-loading port on the Baltic. It also serves as the endpoint of the Baltic Pipeline System, making it a crucial strategic hub for Russian energy exports.

Despite air defenses taking down several drones, enough managed to penetrate the defenses and ignite fuel tanks, causing a fire visible from miles away. Over 100 flights were delayed or canceled at St Petersburg's Pulkovo airport, leaving passengers stranded overnight in the terminal.

In another setback for Putin, Vyacheslav Gladkov, the governor of his Belgorod region, was "targeted by a Ukrainian drone" during a meeting with residents but managed to escape unharmed.

Separately, Ukraine's third army successfully thwarted a major Russian attempt to breach the frontline around Lyman. Russian troops launched offensives across seven sectors but are said to have sustained significant casualties.

Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky accused Russian military leaders of misleading Putin regarding battlefield achievements.

"We've noted attempts by the Russians to intensify their offensive efforts, taking advantage of more favourable weather," the Ukrainian leader stated.

"As a result, the only tangible outcome for the Russian army has been an increase in their losses, namely more than 8,000 killed and seriously wounded Russian soldiers in just these seven days.

"We also have information that the Russian command has finally managed to figure out where their units are actually located on the frontline and how this differs from the official reports that have been coming in.

"Some of their brigade-level commanders have already been replaced as punishment for their lies."
