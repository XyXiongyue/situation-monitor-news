Europe’s diesel rebuild leans on US refining

2026-05-26 18:36

inOil & Companies News26/05/2026

Seaborne diesel/gasoil imports into Europe are recovering in May after hitting a decade low in April. Diesel/gasoil exports to Europe from outside regions rose to 1.5mbd in May (days 1-18), up 16% y-o-y, despite supplies from the usual East of Suez markets, Middle East Gulf and India West Coast, down to near zero. The y-o-y gain from US flows alone exceeds the East of Suez shortfall, leaving Europe with more external supply this May than a year ago.

Europe finds diesel without East of SuezTotal diesel/gasoil exports to Europe from outside regions rose to 1.5mbd in May (days 1-18), up 50% from the decade lows seen in March and April and 200kbd above the same period last year. The East of Suez supply channel has effectively closed, with Middle East Gulf exports to Europe down to ~5kbd in early May from 110kbd a year earlier. Volumes from India West Coast fell to zero from 190kbd – a combined 295kbd y-o-y loss.

Atlantic Basin has reorganised around the gap, with PADD 1 and PADD 3 diesel/gasoil exports to Europe rising by 400kbd y-o-y, more than covering the East of Suez loss on their own. Red Sea Gulf of Aden exports added 40kbd, and Canada East Coast 50kbd. EU sanctions earlier this year on Indian and Turkish diesel refined from Russian crude had already begun thinning the East of Suez supply channel before the Hormuz closure removed what was left.

ARA gasoil inventories sit 18% below year-ago levels and declined throughout April and May , leaving Europe’s stock cover thin even as the import rebuild gathers pace (Insights Global via Argus).

US refining carries the substitutionTotal US PADD 3 and PADD 1 diesel/gasoil exports reached 1.8mbd in May (days 1-18), 44% above the strongest export month of 2025 at 1.25mbd in June. PADD 3 refinery utilisation reached 97% in mid-May (EIA), around 5pp above the same week a year earlier, after the US administration asked refiners to shorten planned spring maintenance to maximise summer supply.

PADD 1 and PADD 3 diesel/gasoil exports to Europe more than doubled to 625kbd in early May from 225kbd a year earlier, with the Europe share of US exports climbing to 35% from 20% in May 2025, the highest in the dataset. The y-o-y gain from US flows alone exceeds the loss from East of Suez refiners, leaving Europe with more external supply this May than a year ago on the strength of the US response.

US volumes to LatAm also rose by 250kbd y-o-y in May, up to 1mbd. While LatAm’s share fell to 53%, down from 61% last year, that share could shift back into summer, with Brazil’s harvest season lifting diesel demand and constrained Russia diesel exports limiting alternative supplies.

The Atlantic Basin’s diesel rebalance currently rests on US refining holding its dataset-high export pace through peak summer demand, followed by hurricane season, and on US barrels continuing to favour Europe over alternatives such as LatAm. With ARA stocks drawing quickly and East of Suez supply effectively closed, disruptions to US output would expose Europe quickly.Source: Vortexa
