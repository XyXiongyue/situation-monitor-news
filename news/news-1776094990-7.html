Iraq leads OPEC+ oil output drop

Mon, 13 Apr 2026 08:33

The logo of the OPEC is pictured at the OPEC headquarters in Vienna, Austria, Photo: AFP

Baghdad (IraqiNews.com) – Iraq led the list of OPEC+ nations that reduced oil output, with crude oil production dropping by around 2.56 million barrels per day to 1.62 million.

OPEC+ oil production fell by approximately 7.7 million barrels per day in March 2026, impacted by reduced supplies from Iraq, Saudi Arabia, and the UAE due to the repercussions of the US-Israeli war on Iran and the closure of the Strait of Hormuz.

According to the organization’s monthly report issued on April 13, the total output from OPEC+ member countries fell to 35.05 million barrels per day last month compared to 42.75 million barrels per day in April.

According to the report, output in OPEC’s 12 member nations fell by 7.87 million barrels per day in March, bringing the total to 20.78 million compared to 28.66 million in February.

The decline follows a decision by Saudi Arabia and seven other OPEC+ members to boost oil output starting in May 2026. This decision comes amid escalating tensions due to regional conflicts and attacks on energy installations in the region.

Due to recent developments, five OPEC+ countries—Saudi Arabia, the UAE, Bahrain, Iraq, and Kuwait—have cut their oil production following disruptions to exports through the Strait of Hormuz.

Saudi Arabia’s oil output decreased by approximately 2.31 million barrels per day, dropping to 7.79 million barrels per day, which marks its lowest level since June 2020.

The UAE’s crude oil production declined by approximately 1.52 million barrels per day, resulting in a total output of 1.89 million barrels per day.

Kuwait’s overall crude oil output decreased by 1.36 million barrels per day, bringing its total production down to 1.21 million barrels per day.
