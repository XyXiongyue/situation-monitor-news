Oil prices fall after US, Iran sign interim peace agreement

2026-06-19 05:56

inOil & Companies News19/06/2026

Oil prices fell on Thursday morning, with Brent crude trading at below $79/barrel, after the US and Iran signed an interim deal to end their war and reopen the Strait of Hormuz.

US President Donald Trump, who is in France for the G7 summit, formally signed the memorandum of understanding (MoU) with Iran in Versailles, according to a White House social media post.

Iran’s foreign ministry said the agreement was already in effect 17 June after Iranian President Masoud Pezeshkian digitally signed the memorandum.

Iran’s parliamentary speaker Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf, however, told Iranian state TV on Thursday that the Strait of Hormuz “will not return to pre-war conditions” after the 60-day ceasefire.

Talks on key points of the deal are expected to begin on 19 June in Geneva, Switzerland, at a separate meeting hosted by mediator Pakistan, including Tehran’s nuclear enrichment program and the status of US sanctions on the Middle Eastern country.

The 14-point agreement extends a ceasefire announced in April by another 60 days to allow the two countries to push talks about Iran’s nuclear program that could be extended by “mutual consent”.

The deal includes an immediate end to the war on all fronts, including Lebanon, the full resumption of maritime traffic in the Strait of Hormuz, the lifting of a US blockade of Iranian ports, the waiving of US sanctions on Iran, the unfreezing of Iranian assets, and a plan worth $300 billion for the economic rehabilitation of Iran, according to media reports.

The deal comes nearly four months after the US and Israel launched the war on Iran on 28 February, effectively closing the Strait of Hormuz, which is a chokepoint for a ‌fifth of ⁠the world’s oil and liquefied natural gas (LNG) supplies.

On the supply front, US crude oil inventories fell for the 10th straight week on 12 June as demand surged, pushing total stockpiles to their lowest level since 1985 as the Iran war continues to upend global energy markets, data from the US Energy Information Administration showed on 17 June.
