Drewry: Cancelled Sailings Lower Last Week

2026-06-30 18:17

inInternational Shipping News30/06/2026

Our weekly Cancelled Sailings Tracker provides a snapshot of blank sailings announced by each Alliance versus the total number of scheduled sailings.

Weekly analysis: 26 Jun 2026Although carriers are cancelling fewer sailings, schedules are stabilising and the Strait of Hormuz is gradually reopening, freight rates continue to rise.

Across the major East–West trades, only 24 blank sailings are expected over the next five weeks, from week 27 (29 June-5 July) to week 31 (27 July-2 August), representing a cancellation rate of just 3%, with 97% of scheduled services still expected to operate. Most disruptions remain concentrated on the Transpacific eastbound trade (63%), followed by Asia–Europe/Med (29%), while the Transatlantic (8%) remains comparatively unaffected. Gemini Cooperation and MSC continue to demonstrate strong schedule adherence.

The improving operational backdrop suggests supply conditions are gradually improving. However, strong cargo demand, frontloading activity and tight vessel availability continue to underpin freight rates across the major trade lanes. As of 25 June, Drewry’s World Container Index (WCI) rose 5% WoW to $4,166 per 40ft container, its highest level since September 2024. Transpacific rates increased 8%, Asia–North Europe/Med rates gained 1% and Transatlantic rates rose 3%.

For shippers, improving conditions in Hormuz and rising capacity are unlikely to bring immediate freight cost relief, as strong demand continues to keep vessel space tight and freight rates firm through the peak season.Source: Drewry
