BofA sees Brazil rate hikes possible in 2027 amid Fed shift

2026-06-26 05:15

inWorld Economy News26/06/2026

Bank of America says Brazil’s central bank has likely completed its rate-cutting cycle and will hold the Selic rate at 14.25% for an extended period, according to a research note released Thursday.

The firm says the probability distribution has shifted, with the risk that the next move is no longer a cut but eventually a hike in 2027 becoming non-negligible. The latest Copom cut was accompanied by a much more restrictive message, Bank of America notes.

The external backdrop has turned less supportive after Bank of America economists changed their Federal Reserve call to three 25-basis-point hikes in 2026. The new forecast takes the federal funds rate to 4.25% to 4.50%, with rates then held through 2027.

That combination materially reduces the room for renewed easing in Brazil, the firm says. Bank of America adds the shift raises the odds that the BCB may need to retighten if inflation convergence stalls.

The Banco Central do Brasil previously cut its benchmark Selic rate to 14.25% at its most recent policy meeting.Source: Investing.com
