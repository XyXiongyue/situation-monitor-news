Crude supply recovery through H2 hinges on peace deal, 2027 to see upswing – IEA

2026-06-19 18:15

inOil & Companies News19/06/2026

Crude supply could gradually recover through the second half of the year the International Energy Agency (IEA) said on Wednesday, if a peace deal between the US and Iran is struck.

Supply is projected to fall by 3.9 million barrels/day to 102.4 million barrels/day in 2026, after output dropped to 94.5 million barrels/day in May, down 600,000 barrels/day on the previous month. This was 13.6 million barrels/day lower than pre-conflict levels, according to the IEA June Oil Market Report.

However, increasing shipments through the Strait of Hormuz in early June, supported by ship-to-ship transfers in the Gulf of Oman, have already lifted total flows to 12 million barrels/day, from a low of 9.6 million barrels/day in May, the IEA said.

Removing mines from shipping lanes and smoothing out kinks in the supply chain will take time, but oil supply is forecast to ratchet up 8 million barrels/day in 2027, rising to 110.3 million barrels/day.

Supply disruptions in the wake of the Middle East conflict will be partly offset by more material available from non-OPEC+ producers, with the IEA citing robust growth in the Americas and a release of US oil reserves.

Stocks have taken a significant hit in recent months, with OECD government inventories falling by 163 million barrels (down 1.8 milion barrels/day) to the lowest level since December 1990 as emergency stock releases accelerated.

Global oil reserves fell by 143 million barrels in May (4.6 million barrels/day) compared with 74 million barrels (2.5 million barrels/day) in April.

The average pace of stock draws since the start of the war rose to 3.8 million barrels/day, comprised of 2.4 million barrels/day for crude, and 1.4 million barrels/day for refined products.

“Further declines in the coming months could still take global oil stocks to historic lows before the market balance shifts to surplus towards the end of the year,” said the IEA.

Refinery throughput rates are expected to fall by 2 million barrels/day in 2026 to 82 million barrels/day, driven by a 4.7 million barrels/day drop in the second quarter. This is forecast to rise by 3.1 million barrels/day in 2027, to an average of 85 million barrels/day.

Crude imports into China and Japan fell by 40%, with lower refinery crude runs in China, the Middle East, Eurasia and elsewhere in Asia eroding demand, leading the IEA to cut its crude run estimates for 2026 by 370,000 barrels/day.

Demand in 2026 is forecast to fall by 1.1 million barrels/day compared with 2025, down 700,000 barrels/day compared to the IEA’s May report.

Deliveries in the second quarter dropped 5 million barrels/day year on year as higher prices and tight supply weighed on appetite.
