Rubio Warns US Has 'Another Way' if Iran Nuclear Talks Collapse

Mon, 25 May 2026 06:24

U.S. officials say talks are focused on stabilizing energy flows while addressing Iran's nuclear program in later stages of negotiations.

U.S. Secretary of State Marco Rubio said Monday that Washington is pursuing a "strong agreement" with Iran while keeping pressure measures in place if negotiations fail to deliver results.

Speaking during a press briefing in New Delhi, Rubio said the United States remains open to diplomacy but will continue enforcing sanctions until any deal is fully finalized and verified.

He said there is "a pretty solid thing on the table" involving discussions on maritime access through the Strait of Hormuz and broader talks over Iran's nuclear program. President Donald Trump has also indicated that U.S. pressure on Tehran will remain in place until clear progress is made in negotiations.

Iranian state-affiliated media Tasnim News Agency reported that significant gaps remain between the two sides, including Tehran's demand for the release of frozen assets abroad. Iranian parliamentary spokesperson Ebrahim Rezaei said Iran would respond to pressure with "action for action" and would not accept threats.

Oil Prices Fall on Signs of Diplomatic Movement

Oil prices dropped sharply following reports of possible diplomatic progress between Washington and Tehran. Brent crude fell nearly 6% to around $97 per barrel, according to market data.

Traders reacted to signals that negotiations could ease risks to shipping routes through the Strait of Hormuz, a critical chokepoint that carries roughly 20 million barrels of oil and fuel exports daily.

Reuters, citing U.S. officials familiar with the talks, reported that Iran has agreed in principle to discuss reopening shipping access through the Strait of Hormuz and limiting aspects of its enriched uranium stockpile in exchange for potential sanctions relief.

The report said discussions also include a possible 60-day framework for broader negotiations. However, officials stressed that major disagreements remain unresolved.

Nuclear and Sanctions Issues Remain Central

U.S. officials say talks are focused on stabilizing energy flows while addressing Iran's nuclear program in later stages of negotiations.

The International Atomic Energy Agency has previously reported that Iran continues to enrich uranium up to 60% purity, close to weapons-grade levels, raising concerns among Western governments.

Iran has continued to seek sanctions relief and access to frozen overseas funds while maintaining its own conditions for any agreement.

Iran Signals Reciprocal Response Strategy

Iran has also engaged diplomatic channels with China, Russia and regional mediators as talks continue, according to Iranian officials.

Despite signs of engagement, no final agreement has been reached, and major differences remain over sanctions relief, nuclear limits and regional security guarantees.
