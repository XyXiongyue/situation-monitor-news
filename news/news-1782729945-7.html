US refining capacity drops 1.43% in 2025, EIA reports

2026-06-29 18:45

inOil & Companies News29/06/2026

US refining capacity decreased by 263,000 barrels per day in 2025, representing a 1.43% decline, according to a report released by the US Energy Information Administration. The reduction resulted from the planned conversion of a major Houston refinery and the closure of a Los Angeles-area facility.

Marathon Petroleum, headquartered in Findlay, Ohio, maintains its position as the nation’s largest refiner with 2.986 million bpd, accounting for 16.4% of national capacity, the EIA data showed.

Valero Energy Corp, based in San Antonio, ranks second with 2.23 million bpd, representing 12% of US capacity. The Saudi Aramco-owned Motiva Enterprises facility in Port Arthur, Texas, remains the country’s largest single refinery with a capacity of 656,400 bpd. The Port Arthur plant increased its capacity by 15,900 bpd through improved refining efficiency.

The EIA report draws from capacity data submitted by individual refineries to the agency by January 1, 2026.

Refinery capacity fluctuations occur regularly on a year-to-year basis, with capacity often recovering as facilities enhance operating efficiency.

Chemical manufacturer Lyondell Basell Industries closed its 263,776 bpd Houston refinery in February 2025 to convert the site into a petrochemical plant. The company stated the refinery no longer aligned with its business model as a global chemical producer.

Phillips 66 shut down its 138,700 bpd Los Angeles-area refinery in October 2025, citing uncertain viability due to changing market dynamics in California.Source: Investing.com
