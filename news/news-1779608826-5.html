Iran embassy in India rejects Rubio’s claims, blames US sanctions for energy and regional ...

Sun, 24 May 2026 00:33

Iran’s embassy in India rejected US Secretary of State Marco Rubio’s remarks on Tehran, blaming American sanctions and regional policies for global energy instability while defending Iran’s nuclear programme as peaceful and warning that its armed forces remain prepared against any threat.

The Embassy of the Islamic Republic of Iran in India has strongly pushed back against remarks made by US Secretary of State Marco Rubio, accusing Washington of attempting to shift attention away from what it described as the destabilising role of the United States and the “Zionist regime” in West Asia.

The response came afterRubio, during discussions with PM Narendra Modion Saturday, said the United States would not allow Iran to “hold the global energy market hostage.” According to US State Department spokesperson Tommy Pigott, Rubio also highlighted the possibility of American energy exports helping diversify India’s energy supplies amid continuing tensions in the region.

US sanctions for oil supply and energy resources

In a detailed statement issued on May 24, theIranian Embassy in India argued that Tehran has consistently been willing to supply oil and energy resources to countries around the world, including India. The Embassy maintained that the real disruption to global energy markets has come from US sanctions targeting Iranian oil exports, describing the measures as unlawful and contrary to international law and the United Nations Charter.

The statement further alleged that sanctions imposed by US over the past several decades have gone beyond the energy sector and affected access to medicines and critical medical equipment for Iranian citizens. According to the Embassy, such restrictions have contributed to widespread humanitarian suffering and endangered the lives of patients in need of treatment.

Iran also accused theUnited States and Israel of creating instability in the region through military and provocative actions, particularly in and around the Strait of Hormuz.Tehran claimed these actions have endangered maritime security and intensified the global energy crisis.

Responding to concerns over its nuclear activities, Iran reiterated that its nuclear programme remains peaceful and operates under the supervision of the International Atomic Energy Agency. The Embassy said Iran, as a member of the Treaty on the Non-Proliferation of Nuclear Weapons, considers access to peaceful nuclear technology a legitimate and internationally recognised right.

The issue ofIran’s nuclear programmehas remained central to ongoing diplomatic negotiations involving the United States, Israel and Iran, and continues to be a major sticking point in efforts to secure a broader ceasefire agreement.

The Embassy also asserted that Iran had resisted pressure and military aggression from the United States and Israel, claiming Tehran emerged stronger despite attempts to weaken its political system. It added that Iran’s armed forces remain fully prepared to respond to any threat against the country’s sovereignty and territorial integrity.
