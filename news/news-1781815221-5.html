谁为特朗普伊朗协议中的3000亿美元重建基金提供资金？

Thu, 18 Jun 2026 13:08

华盛顿--唐纳德·特朗普总统在宣传与伊朗新签署的谅解备忘录时，批评前总统巴拉克·奥巴马向伊朗提供17亿美元“绿色现金”，作为特朗普撕毁的2015年伊朗核协议的一部分。

然而，特朗普结束美国在伊朗战争的初步协议因以3000亿美元的重建基金的形式为伊朗提供了可能更大的意外之财而受到审查。

特朗普6月17日与伊朗签署的谅解备忘录引发了人们对伊朗重建基金的资金来源以及美国是否最终会因此而陷入困境的质疑。特朗普坚称不会向该基金投入任何美元，但他尚未确定哪些国家或私人实体将承担这些费用。

更多：美国、伊朗开始最终和平谈判：实时战争更新

根据该协议，美国及其中东伙伴将“至少与美国制定一项最终的、双方同意的计划”根据谅解备忘录的文本，3000亿美元用于伊朗伊斯兰共和国的重建和经济发展。

美国还将解除对伊朗的所有制裁并解冻其资产，但前提是伊朗履行不发展核武器和处置高浓缩铀的承诺，特朗普政府希望在60天内达成最终协议。特朗普签署谅解备忘录后，美国立即解除了对伊朗石油出口的限制。

该基金旨在激励伊朗与美国完成最终协议，并在伊朗在战争期间遭受重创后启动经济发展和基础设施改善。该基金的实施机制将在与伊朗达成的最终协议中最终确定，伊朗必须遵守和平协议的条款才能从中获得资金。

然而，该文本没有明确说明哪些国家捐款，也没有明确说明私营部门捐款是否会为该基金捐款。特朗普在法国举行的七国集团会议上否认美国会提供任何资金。

“我们不会投资。我们不会拿出10美分，”特朗普6月17日表示。“人们可以决定这样做，但这取决于他们。"

"There is no 300 Billion Dollar payment to Iran by the U.S. That's Fake News!"Trump later said in a post on Truth Social. "All there is for the U.S. is Success, Lower Oil Prices, and Victory."

Will the $300 billion fund be covered by private donors?

Vice PresidentJD Vancemade the same commitment as he addressed reporters at a June 18 White House briefing with reporters. "The United States isn't giving up a cent of money, and even the economic benefits, the sanctions relief that comes along with this bargain, only happens if the Iranians perform."

But if the U.S. isn't paying into the fund, who is?

Reuters, citing an unnamed source familiar with the deal,reported that more than half of the fund's $300 billionhas already been committed and that it will be comprised entirely of private-sector dollars.

Companies based in the U.S., the Gulf Arab states, Asia, South America and Africa have committed to financing, according to the Reuters report, which said the administration views the fund as a private investment vehicle, not a government reconstruction program.

Vance suggested private investors and Iran's neighboring countries would be putting up the cash, though he didn't identify any specific sources.

"There is a great desire from the Arab world and from outside the Arab world to actually get involved in Iran if they behave properly," Vance said when asked to identify who would be covering the redevelopment fund.

Vance pointed to the United Arab Emirates as an example. "Let's just say that they would like to invest in building a power plant. That is actually impossible right now because of the way that U.S. sanctions work," the vice president said. "So what we're saying is that if you behave and if the Emirates themselves want to build a power plant, then we will do the sanctions relief necessary to make that possible."

Vance added that discussions about the fund's funding sources are preliminary because Iran must first agree to the nuclear terms in a final agreement.

"This is so far in advance because it assumes a transformation in Iranian behavior," he said.

For years, Trump has attacked Obama for a $1.7 billion cash payment to Iran. The transaction, however, was the result of the Obama administration's 2016 settlement with Iran ending a decades-long legal dispute after Iran never received military equipment that it paid the U.S. $400 million for before the 1979 Iranian revolution.

Trump also routinely accused Obama of giving Iran $150 billion as part of his nuclear deal with Iran. Yet Obama's deal did not involve direct payments to Iran, but instead ‒ similar to Trump's preliminary agreement ‒unfroze Iran's access to its own cashin foreign bank accounts, totaling about $50 billion.

Nevertheless, Trump accused Obama of handing a "bribe" to Iran to get it to sign off on his nuclear deal.

"He tried to bribe his way out. I didn't do that," Trump said of Obama at this week's G7 summit in France. "And you know what the Iranians did? They laughed at Obama, and they said, 'He's a stupid son of a b----.'"
