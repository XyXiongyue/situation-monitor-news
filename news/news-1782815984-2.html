Oil prices inch down as U.S.-Iran talks temper Hormuz supply concerns

2026-06-30 18:39

inOil & Companies News30/06/2026

Oil prices edged lower on Tuesday as investors weighed prospects for renewed U.S.-Iran talks against lingering concerns over supply disruptions following fresh military exchanges between the two countries.

At 04:56 ET (08:56 GMT), Brent crude futures, the global oil benchmark, fell 0.6% to $72.69 a barrel, while U.S. West Texas Intermediate crude futures fell 0.5% to $70.41 a barrel.

Oil markets remained on edge after Washington and Tehran reportedly halted a flare-up in fighting in the Strait of Hormuz which threatened to derail an interim peace agreement signed earlier this month. Oil prices sank sharply in the wake of the memorandum of understanding and are now sitting around pre-war levels.

President Donald Trump has said that the two sides would resume peace talks in Doha later on Tuesday. But Tehran was yet to commit to more negotiations with the U.S. this week, leaving markets uncertain over whether negotiations would proceed and the current status of the Strait of Hormuz.

White House officials have stressed that the vital waterway for a fifth of the world’s oil is now open to tanker traffic. But Iran’s Deputy Foreign Minister Kazem Gharibabadi said Tehran would continue pursuing plans to jointly oversee maritime traffic through the strait even if nearby Oman chooses not to participate. The prospect of greater Iranian control over traffic through the strait could slow the recovery of crude exports from the Persian Gulf, ANZ analysts said, adding that persistent shipping risks continue to cloud the region’s supply outlook.

The ANZ analysts added that while crude prices have largely retraced their conflict-driven gains, tighter refined fuel markets continue to point to underlying supply constraints. The divergence between softer crude prices and firmer fuel markets suggests refinery margins should remain supported even if benchmark crude prices stay subdued.Source: Investing.com
