US-Iran War Live Updates: IAEA to resume Iran nuclear inspections after US-Iran accord

The UN nuclear watchdog will soon resume inspections in Iran following the interim agreement reached between the United States and Iran, with discussions on the timing and procedures set to begin shortly, IAEA chief Rafael Grossi said.

The move follows a 14-point memorandum of understanding signed last week that opened a 60-day negotiation period on key issues, including Iran’s nuclear programme. Iran has barred the IAEA from its most sensitive nuclear sites since US and Israeli strikes last year, while all inspections were suspended after the attacks on February 28.

Hemmati further emphasized that Iran would retain flexibility over both the transportation of its oil and the currency used for payments, noting that export proceeds need not be settled exclusively in US dollars, according to the Al Jazeera report.

US-Iran War Live Updates: Strait of Hormuz ship traffic surges as transit confidence returns

Vessel traffic through the Strait of Hormuz rebounded sharply over two consecutive weekends, with confirmed crossings rising from 32 vessels between 12-14 June to 93 vessels between 19-21 June. The most significant increase occurred on Saturday, June 20 when crossings jumped from just three to 42 compared with the previous weekend.

Data from MarineTraffic and Kpler suggests shipping activity is recovering through the vital maritime route. The rebound has been supported by recent diplomatic developments and a temporary OFAC general license, which has reduced compliance concerns for approved Hormuz transits until 21 August.
