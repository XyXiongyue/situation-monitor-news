Iran war could cost US $1 trillion, far above official estimates: Harvard expert

The ongoing Iran war could impose a far heavier financial burden on the United States than currently disclosed, according to analysis by Linda Bilmes. While the Pentagon has pegged the first six days of operations at $11.3 billion, Bilmes argues the real cost is already significantly higher and could eventually approach $1 trillion.

Her research highlights how official figures often understate expenses by valuing military assets at historical cost rather than current replacement prices. She estimates daily costs at roughly $2 billion during active combat, with hidden expenses pushing early totals closer to $16 billion.

Long-term costs could escalate further due to expensive replenishment contracts with firms like Lockheed Martin and Boeing. These asymmetries, she warns, could strain US public finances well beyond the battlefield.
