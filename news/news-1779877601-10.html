Indian refiners process less crude in April amid Middle East supply disruptions

2026-05-27 18:26

inOil & Companies News27/05/2026

Indian refiners processed 5.23 million barrels per day of crude oil in April, marking an 8.9% decline from March’s 5.55 million barrels per day, according to provisional government data released on Tuesday.

The April throughput, equivalent to 21.39 million metric tons, also represented a 2.2% decrease compared to the same month last year. March’s refinery throughput stood at 23.48 million metric tons.

India ranks as the world’s third-largest oil importer and consumer.

The U.S.-Iran conflict has caused volatility in energy prices, with shipping flows through the Strait of Hormuz remaining disrupted. The strait serves as a passage for approximately 20% of global oil and liquefied natural gas supplies.

Indian refiners have shifted to imports from Latin America and Africa following disruptions to Middle East supplies, according to data from trade sources.

Nayara Energy, partially owned by Russian oil company Rosneft (MCX:ROSN), received only one Russian oil cargo delivery in April. The refiner had completely shut down its facility for most of the month to conduct routine maintenance, ship tracking data from industry sources showed.Source: Investing.com
