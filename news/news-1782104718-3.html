Fighting in Lebanon has been on hold for the past 48 hours, according to Lebanon Army Radio, Bloomberg reported.

The development comes as mediators seek to preserve the ceasefire arrangements outlined in the US-Iran memorandum of understanding, with a new de-confliction mechanism for Lebanon agreed during talks in Switzerland.

US-Iran War Live Updates: Iran says Lebanon war must end as negotiations move to next phase

Iranian Foreign Ministry spokesperson Esmaeil Baghaei said the war must end on all fronts, including Lebanon, as negotiators work on implementing the US-Iran memorandum of understanding.

Baghaei said discussions covered all clauses of the agreement, including oil exports and the release of frozen Iranian assets.

He also indicated that talks continued through Qatari and Pakistani mediators after the four-party format was halted following recent threats by US President Donald Trump.

US-Iran War Live Updates: Israel to lift all restrictions in northern border areas from Monday

Israel’s military said all restrictions in communities along the country’s northern border with Lebanon will be lifted from 6 am on Monday, signalling a further easing of security measures amid efforts to maintain the ceasefire.

“Starting at 6:00 am on Monday, June 22, 2026, all restrictions will be lifted in the Confrontation Line area,” the military said in a statement.

It added that border communities would move to a “full activity level, with no restrictions” after previously operating under a partial activity level.

US-Iran War Live Updates: Araghchi says footballers, negotiators and soldiers are part of the same fight

Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi on Monday shared an image depicting schoolchildren reaching for a football on a pitch, accompanied by the hashtag #Minab168, a reference to children killed during the conflict.

In a post on X, Araghchi linked Iran’s diplomatic efforts, sporting achievements and military actions to what he described as a broader national cause.

“From the football pitch to the negotiating table to the battlefield, every step we take as Iranians is part of a larger struggle: defending the honor and dignity of our dear people,” Araghchi wrote.

The image comes as Iranian officials continue to invoke the memory of those killed during the war while negotiations with the United States move into a new phase in Switzerland.
