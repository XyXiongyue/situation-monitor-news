Oil extends falls as supply worries ease; China petrochemical futures slump

2026-06-26 05:17

inOil & Companies News26/06/2026

Crude oil prices extended declines on Thursday, with US crude falling below $70/barrel, pulling along China’s petrochemical futures markets, on prospects of supply returning from the Middle East.

At 03:00 GMT, front-month WTI futures shed 1.5% to $69.26/barrel and Brent fell by 1.6% to $72.54/barrel, practically erasing risk premiums from the US-Iran war.

Shipping through the Strait of Hormuz has ramped up following progress of the US-Iran peace talks, easing the war-triggered supply crunch of oil and petrochemicals.

In China’s domestic petrochemical futures markets, prices of commodities were tracking crude losses on Thursday morning, led by purified terephthalic acid (PTA), which slumped by more than 5%.

With de-escalation of geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, China’s overcapacity issues are coming back to the fore.

This, coupled with anticipated supply increase from the Middle East and Asia, are expected to weigh on China petrochemical markets.
