Mainport Shipping Marks Geo Master Christening As Hybrid Survey Vessel Enters Operational Service

2026-06-24 19:50

inInternational Shipping News24/06/2026

Hybrid survey and ROV support vessel enters operational service following christening in IJmuiden under long-term charter arrangement

LEADMainport Shipping, the Cork-based Irish maritime company, today marks the christening of the Geo Master, a hybrid survey and ROV support vessel delivered by Neptune Marine and set to enter operational service under a long-term commercial charter arrangement with N-Sea following its ceremony in IJmuiden.

The vessel strengthens Mainport Shipping’s focus on the ownership and management of modern offshore assets supporting subsea and offshore energy operations across Europe and the United Kingdom.

VESSEL & PROJECT OVERVIEWThe Geo Master has been constructed by Neptune Marine in Hardinxveld-Giessendam and delivered following completion of its build programme. The vessel will now enter operational service under a long-term commercial charter arrangement with N-Sea.

Designed as a next-generation hybrid survey and ROV support vessel, the Geo Master will be deployed across offshore survey, subsea inspection, and infrastructure-related projects within the offshore energy sector.

The project forms part of a long-term collaboration between Mainport Shipping and N-Sea, combining asset ownership and technical ship management with operational deployment in the offshore market.

MAINPORT ROLEMainport Shipping is the owner and ship manager of the Geo Master.The vessel is operated under a long-term commercial charter arrangement with N-Sea.

TECHNOLOGY & EFFICIENCYThe Geo Master is based on the proven design of the Geo Ranger, further developed to enhance operational efficiency and performance in demanding offshore environments.

The vessel features Dynamic Positioning Class 2 (DP2) capability, supporting precise station keeping and enhanced operational flexibility during offshore survey and subsea operations.

The vessel also features a hybrid propulsion configuration designed to optimise fuel consumption and improve efficiency across a wide range of offshore activities.

Furthermore, the Geo Master is compatible with alternative fuels, including biofuel blends, supporting flexible fuel strategies and contributing to lower-impact offshore operations where feasible.

These characteristics support efficient deployment across offshore energy and infrastructure projects, including offshore renewable energy developments, where operational efficiency, reliability and environmental performance are increasingly important.

INDUSTRY CONTEXTThrough its partnership with N-Sea, the Geo Master will be deployed across offshore energy and subsea infrastructure projects in Europe and the United Kingdom, supporting demand for advanced survey and ROV capabilities across both renewable and conventional offshore markets.
