EcoMarine Index Identifies Sustainability Leaders Across the Black and Caspian Seas

2026-06-30 18:23

inInternational Shipping News30/06/2026

At the end of 2025, Kelp Farms launched the EcoMarine Index, an independent international ESG index designed to objectively assess the environmental impact of human activities across various marine regions of the world’s oceans.

The Index establishes a transparent and universal framework for evaluating the sustainability of marine resource use by combining scientific data, sustainable development principles, and applied analytics.

EcoMarine Index was created to improve ocean health and coastal resilience by delivering independent, systematic assessments of environmental impacts created by marine stakeholders, and by translating those findings into practical signals for better decisions, investment, and regulation.

“On World Oceans Day, I applaud the launch of the EcoMarine Index and its commitment to advancing responsible ocean stewardship and transparency across the Black Sea, Caspian, and other vital marine regions. Healthy seas are essential to the well-being of all life on Earth, and regional leadership is critical to building resilience in the face of accelerating climate and biodiversity challenges. Initiatives that encourage collaboration, science-based action, and long-term care for marine ecosystems are more important than ever. I am encouraged to see growing momentum for protecting the ocean — our blue heart — through informed and cooperative efforts such as this.” said Dr. Sylvia Earle, renowned marine biologist, ocean explorer, founder of Mission Blue, and one of the world’s leading advocates for ocean conservation.

The EcoMarine Index creates opportunities for dialogue between science, business, and society while providing a platform for transforming marine ecosystem challenges into practical environmental solutions and actions.

“The creation of a unified system for assessing environmental improvements in marine ecosystems can become an important tool for sharing experience among cities, ports, companies, and environmental projects around the world,” said Sergey Pavlyuk, Strategic Change Support Expert at IFL Entrepreneur (Swedish Institute of Management).

The 2026 pilot assessment includes shipping companies, offshore oil and gas operators, and coastal cities. The pilot study covers the Black Sea and Caspian Sea regions.

“The results of the pilot EcoMarine Index assessment for the Black and Caspian Seas demonstrated a relatively high level of engagement among businesses and municipalities in the sustainability agenda, with leaders and best practices identified in almost every country. The average Index value reached 0.94 for shipping companies, 1.11 for offshore oil and gas operators, and 1.01 for coastal cities,” said Olga Lakustova, Director of Kelp Farms.

Among shipping companies, the Azerbaijan Caspian Shipping Company (ASCO) achieved the highest Index score due to its strong transparency practices, followed by Turkey’s Arkas Line and Kazakhstan’s Kazmortransflot (KMTF), recognized for their sustainability reporting, biodiversity conservation efforts, and environmental initiatives.

“KazMunayGas (Kazakhstan) led the ranking among offshore oil and gas operators, followed by NCOC (Kazakhstan), Romgaz (Romania), SOCAR (Azerbaijan), and OMV Petrom (Romania). These companies demonstrated strong transparency and commitment to responsible marine management. At the same time, major operators such as TPAO (Turkey) and AIOC (Azerbaijan) have the potential to strengthen their positions through more detailed disclosure of environmental performance at the offshore project level,” added Alexandra Lazareva, Head of the EcoMarine Index Project.

“Constanța (Romania), Varna (Bulgaria), and Burgas (Bulgaria) emerged as the leading coastal cities in the Index, benefiting from strong environmental governance frameworks and established sustainability practices. Meanwhile, Baku (Azerbaijan), Aktau (Kazakhstan), Batumi (Georgia), and Samsun (Turkey) are making significant progress by advancing environmental management and promoting community-based environmental initiatives,” explained Alexander Pakhalov, EcoMarine Index expert and co-author of the methodology.

“The pilot assessment results confirm the strong potential for international cooperation in sustainable marine management. Despite differences in environmental governance approaches and disclosure practices, most participants demonstrate a clear commitment to implementing responsible practices and improving transparency.

As the EcoMarine Index continues to develop, it will serve not only as a tool for tracking progress, but also as a platform for sharing best practices in marine ecosystem conservation and the sustainable development of coastal areas.

In 2027, the Index will expand to new marine regions and additional categories of marine stakeholders, creating new opportunities for international collaboration, knowledge sharing, and the wider adoption of effective environmental practices,” concluded Olga Lakustova, Director of Kelp Farms.Source: EcoMarine Index
