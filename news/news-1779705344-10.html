UAE left OPEC to maximize oil revenues before energy transition

2026-05-25 18:35

inOil & Companies News25/05/2026

The United Arab Emirates’ departure from OPEC stemmed from a three-year decision-making process driven by the belief that the world is approaching the end of the oil era, according to a senior adviser to the president.

Anwar Gargash, adviser to UAE President Sheikh Mohamed bin Zayed Al Nahyan, said the country needed to maximize oil revenues while demand remains strong.

The UAE ended its nearly 60-year membership of the Organization of the Petroleum Exporting Countries on May 1.

Gargash said the exit was primarily motivated by OPEC production quotas that kept the country’s output significantly below its capacity.

“We see that we are close to the sort of autumn of the hydrocarbon age,” Gargash said. “And as a result, if you have the ability to produce and generate income and use that income in other investments, that’s what you should do.”

The UAE’s production capacity stands at 4.85 million barrels per day. The country plans to increase capacity to 5 million bpd by 2027. Before leaving OPEC and the wider OPEC+ group, which includes other oil producers led by Russia, its production target was closer to 3.5 million bpd.

The decision is unlikely to affect the market in the immediate term due to Iran’s effective closure of the Strait of Hormuz, but it could impact OPEC’s control over supplies when oil flows normalize.Source: Investing.com
