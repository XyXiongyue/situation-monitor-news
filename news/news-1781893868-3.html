US-Iran War Live Updates: Iran says Strait of Hormuz remains open, Switzerland meeting not urgent after US MoU

Iranian Foreign Ministry spokesperson Esmail Baghaei said the planned meeting in Switzerland was no longer considered urgent after the Iran-US Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) was signed digitally in the early hours of June 18. However, he added that both sides are planning to hold discussions in the coming days as part of the ongoing diplomatic process.

Baghaei also rejected media reports claiming that Iran had closed the Strait of Hormuz, stating that Tehran has taken the necessary steps to ensure the safe passage of commercial vessels and that shipping traffic through the waterway continues uninterrupted. He further noted that under Clause 9 of the MoU, Iran’s nuclear programme will maintain its current status during the 60-day period, with inspections by the International Atomic Energy Agency continuing at facilities such as Bushehr Nuclear Power Plant. Inspections at facilities where access was suspended following what Iran described as US-Israeli military attacks will be addressed through the negotiation process and its outcomes.
